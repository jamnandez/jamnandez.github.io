Personal

Beyond Data Science

While much of this website focuses on my academic and professional work, I also enjoy activities that keep me active and curious!

My interests often revolve around exploration and personal growth—whether that means working through a difficult bouldering route, exploring a new city by bicycle, experimenting in the kitchen, or getting immersed in a well-designed game world.

Hobbies & Interests

Bouldering & Climbing

My first time bouldering was from pure boredom on a random day in study abroad, and I had no idea what climbing really was. Now, it is my favorite hobby, and I the combination of physical strength, technique, and problem-solving that climbing builds. It’s also a great sport for socializing!

Gaming

I really enjoy immersing myself in role-playing and strategy video games, particularly those with strong world-building and player choice. The Elder Scrolls series has long been a favorite, starting with Skyrim as a kid!

Cooking, Baking, & Nutrition

Growing up in a family where food was a love language, I’ve always enjoyed cooking and see it as a way to care for both the body and the spirit.

My favorite thing to bake is rosquillas, a traditional Nicaraguan cookie made from corn, cheese, and panela.

Reading

I read a mix of nonfiction and fiction, viewing both as ways to understand our world.

My nonfiction reading tends to focus on society and public issues. I’m currently reading Pathogenesis, which explores how microbiology has shaped human history and social change.

I also enjoy fiction, especially authors like Gabriel García Márquez, George Orwell, and Suzanne Collins, whose work explores politics, history, and human behavior through literary narrative.

Places & Memories

Louisville, Kentucky

I was born and raised in Louisville, Kentucky, best known for its bourbon, the Kentucky Derby, and fried chicken! While I currently call Minneapolis home, Louisville will always be a key part of my identity.

Minneapolis, Minnesota

Though I moved to Minnesota in 2021 for school, I quickly fell in love with the Twin Cities, and am proud to now call myself a Minnesotan (though I have yet to try Hotdish).

Estelí, Nicaragua

Statues of “Las Gordas” can be found in Estelí, a mountainous northern department of Nicaragua, where my family is from. They celebrate abundance, fertility, and the strength of rural women.

Madrid, Spain

I spent one semester studying at Universidad Carlos III Madrid, and fell in love with one of the most beautiful cities while exploring a very different academic and cultural environment.

Red River Gorge, Kentucky

On one of my trips back home, I was able to climb at the Red River Gorge, one of the most famous climbing destinations. As per tradition, I ate Miguel’s Pizza while drinking Ale-8 Soda - two Kentucky classics.

Choachí, Colombia

Hiking to La Chorrera, Colombia’s tallest waterfall, reminded me how much I enjoy spending time outdoors. Whether close to home or abroad, I always enjoy spending time explore nature.

Vico Equense, Italy

Exploring the coastline around Naples and Mount Vesuvius was incredible, but visiting Pompeii was especially unbelievable. I’ve always been fascinated by history, and seeing an ancient city preserved for nearly two thousand years was an incredible experience.

Hastings, Minnesota

The most effective way to test the quality and freshness of apples at an orchard.
